About

My name is Dragon, and I am a software engineer that grew up on the unceded territory of the Lil'wat, Skwxwú7mesh-ulh Temíx̱w (Squamish) and St̓át̓imc Tmicw (St'at'imc) nation, now known as Whistler, British Columbia. In January 2016, I enrolled in Computer Science at the University of Victoria. Throughout my academic career at UVic, I accumulated many unique experiences. Such as leading UVic Aero's Software Team, which competed annually in the Unmanned Aerial Systems Canada student competition.

During my final university years, I discovered a passion for machine learning and climate change thanks to a handful of courses taught by passionate individuals. The learnings from these courses triggered my career in climate-focused software development, creating building energy auditing software for Audette.io and then later developing satellite image processing pipelines and machine learning models for EarthDaily Analytics.

After my experience working in Earth observation at Earth Daily Analytics, I joined a financial startup company, MazumaGo, where I operated as a backend engineer. Being a part of a lean, fast-paced startup let me experience building software directly for customers. From the ground up, we constructed an online bank by listening closely to customer needs, building fast and releasing faster. faster.

Values

Be Grateful:

We are surrounded by brilliant people who bring amazing things into our projects and personal lives. Let's identify each other's strengths and hard work. Don't let exemplary work go unnoticed!

Release Frequently, Listen Closely:

We build products for the customer; therefore, we must listen closely to their feedback. Always be open minded to the customers feedback and iterate the product fast.

Learn From Your Failures:

We are all human; we make mistakes. With every catastrophic failure comes tremendous learning. Allow your mistakes to improve you and your product, and apply your learnings in the future.

Be Holistic & Detail Oriented:

Although writing robust, scalable software is often integral to a product's development, don't limit your attention to writing code. Understanding the company's mission and product direction may be just as important.
